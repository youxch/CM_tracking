What is Mode Swapping?

When an eigenvalue solver loses track of mode identity, two curves can "swap" — each continuing along the other's trajectory after a crossover frequency.

What is Mode Missing?

Sometimes a mode is simply lost by the solver — its data contains NaN gaps where tracking failed entirely. The curve disappears and reappears unpredictably.
